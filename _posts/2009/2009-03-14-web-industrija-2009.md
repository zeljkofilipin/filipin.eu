The day after [Spletne Urice \#106](/spletne-urice-106/) I spent the whole day at [Web::INDUSTRIJA 2009](http://webindustrija.com/), a small trade fair in Zagreb, Croatia.

Nice people from [linux.hr](http://linux.hr/) got a booth there and asked a few local open source groups if they would like to join them. One of the groups they asked was a group I am member of, [ruby-hr](http://groups.google.com/group/ruby-hr/) (Ruby Croatia).

I planned to be there a few hours, but my boss said it is fine with him if I spend the whole day there, so I went for that option. :)

There was about ten booths, a pretty small trade fair. Linux.hr team were handing out some [Ubuntu](http://www.ubuntu.com/) CDs, and since I do not know anything else, I was showing people how Watir works. I guess I showed Watir to about 10 people that day. Not bad.

Since Ubuntu has live CD, I tried it on my Mac, and it worked fine. Wireless was not working, and Linux gurus said that Ubuntu probably does not have drivers for my hardware.

There is also a funny story. I was entering the fair and saw a big penguin walking around. Of course, it was one of the linux.hr team, so he approached me and said to follow him to our booth. So, I am walking behind a giant penguin, and at the entrance they ask me if I have a ticket. I said: "[I am with the penguin](http://twitter.com/zeljkofilipin/status/1283097605)", and to my surprise they just let me in. I newer thought that knowing a penguin can get you any benefits.

For more information and images, take a look at the [article on linux.hr](http://www.linux.hr/modules/news/article.php?storyid=2725), and there is also a [few twitter posts](http://search.twitter.com/search?q=webindustrija).
