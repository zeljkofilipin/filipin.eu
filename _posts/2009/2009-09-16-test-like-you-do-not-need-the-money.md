[A few days ago](http://twitter.com/zeljkofilipin/status/3685550585) it was exactly 5 years since I started working as a software tester. I planned to publish this on the very date, but life happened.

What happened in the last five years? Am I smarter? Or just older? Let's find out.

**Personal**

![snorkel](/assets/snorkel.JPG "snorkel")

In the last five years I have upgraded LifeOS™ from 26.0 to 31.0, upgraded Girlfriend 5.0 application to Wife 4.0 and installed Son 1.5. I do not use DistanceRunning application so much any more, but I do my best. Drums 1.0 are lost somewhere and Guitar 1.0 is mainly collecting digital dust. No so sure about smarter, but definitely older.

> Smarter vs. Older - 0:1

**Professional**

![zoo](/assets/zoo.jpg "zoo")

Five years ago I had a job I did not like. I thought software (programming to be precise) is so cool, and I wished I could develop it for a living. No luck back then.

![bear](/assets/bear.jpg "bear")

I still think software is lots of fun, and I live in code as much as I can. But, now I think [software testing](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_testing) is even cooler, and I know I was born to be a tester. Not only do I have a job I would do even if I did not have to work, but I get to work from home. A dream I had since I was just a little kid.

> Smarter vs. Older - 1:1

**Programming Languages**

![fire](/assets/fire.jpg "fire")

My [BASIC](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BASIC), [FORTRAN](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fortran) and [PHP](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PHP) skills declined a bit in the last five years, but I am sure I would be up and running in minutes if I ever needed them. (Looks like I had a thing for UPPERCASE languages back then.)  
I have picked up some [Ruby](http://www.ruby-lang.org/en/) and [Watir](http://watir.com/) in the last few years, and I even made it to the [Watir core team](http://watir.com/community/)! Not bad for a simple tester from Croatia, right?! :)

> Smarter vs. Older - 2:1

**Web**

![web](/assets/web.JPG "web")

Five years ago, I had a [crappy site](http://web.archive.org/web/20050205073212/web.vip.hr/zeljko.filipin.vip/) with a crappy URL. (In Croatian only. Some stuff is missing because it is archive.org snapshot, but it did not look much better anyway.)  
Today, I am all over the place. (I even have a place where I try to [aggregate](http://friendfeed.com/zeljkofilipin) my web presence.) I have a [great site](/) with great URL. Five years ago [podcasts](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Podcast) just started to appear, now I have one.

> Smarter vs. Older - 3:1

**Watir**

![sea](/assets/sea.jpg "sea")

I already mentioned [Watir](http://watir.com/), a tool that I use almost every day, and it makes my life much easier. [Big portion](/category/software/watir/) of this site is dedicated to it. I like it so much I talk about [it](/smoke-on-the-watir/) [to](/ruby-on-beers-2/) [anybody](/barcamp-zagreb-2009/) [that](/spletne-urice-106/) [would](/webindustrija-2009/) [listen](/dorscluc-2009/). I even [flew across the ocean](/awta-2009/) to meet with people that like Watir.

**Conclusion**

It really takes a lot of time to write a blog post. No wonder I do not write so much.

Let's take a look at the final score:

> Smarter vs. Older - 3:1

Looks like Smarter won. Maybe another jury would vote differently.

If you remember only one thing from this post, I hope it will be the title:

> Test like you do not need the money.
