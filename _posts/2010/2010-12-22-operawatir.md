For about two years (since [AWTA 2009](http://awta.wikispaces.com/AWTA+2009)) I know that people at [Opera Software](http://www.opera.com/) are working on [OperaWatir](http://operawatir.org/), but the release was scheduled as "soon" all the time. And then when I almost lost any hope, [Andreas Tolf Tolfsen](http://home.e-tjenesten.org/~ato/) published a blog post saying [OperaWatir is (pre)released](http://my.opera.com/core/blog/2010/12/20/operawatir-pre-release)! (One can argue is it a release or prelease, but who cares?)

The source code is available on GitHub at [operasoftware/operawatir](https://github.com/operasoftware/operawatir).

At the moment it works on Windows and GNU/Linux, only on [JRuby](http://jruby.org/) (1.5.1 or above). (Please notice how they say [GNU/Linux](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNU/Linux_naming_controversy), Richard Stallman is probably smiling right now.) Mac is not yet supported.

There are [no plans](http://rubyforge.org/pipermail/wtr-development/2010-December/002735.html) for a [MRI/CRuby](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ruby_MRI) version.

OperaWatir, welcome to the [Watir](http://watir.com/) family! :)
