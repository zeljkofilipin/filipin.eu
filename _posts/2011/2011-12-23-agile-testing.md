[TL;DR](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Too_long;_didn't_read): It is a good book.

I won the [Agile Testing](http://www.agiletester.ca/) book in lottery while I was at [The Austin Workshop on Test Automation](http://awta.wikispaces.com/AWTA+2009) (AWTA) in Austin, Texas, in January 2009. I have met both authors, [Lisa Crispin](http://lisacrispin.com/) and [Janet Gregory](http://janetgregory.ca/), at AWTA and even recorded a podcast about the book. I was planning to read it since then. But, as it usually goes, I had other things to do. I finally decided I will read the book a few months ago, and I was reading it a few pages a day. While reading it, I had to quickly read [another book](/jenkins-the-definitive-guide/), so everything is a bit blurry. A few days ago, I have finally read the book.

Probably the most useful part of the book for me was part 3, the [agile testing quadrants](http://www.exampler.com/old-blog/2003/08/21/). It was introduced by Brian Marick way back in 2003, but I do not think I have heard a lot about it until I found it in the book.

I am pretty bad at book reviews, so I will not go into details. There are a lot of [reviews at Amazon](http://www.amazon.com/Agile-Testing-Practical-Addison-Wesley-Signature/dp/0321534468/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1231516857&sr=1-1), if you like to read them.
