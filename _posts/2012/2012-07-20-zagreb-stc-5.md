Yesterday I was at [Zagreb STC MeetUp](https://github.com/zeljkofilipin/zagreb-stc/wiki) [\#5](http://www.meetup.com/SoftwareTestingClub/events/69947212/). As always, it was organized by [Karlo Šmid](http://zagorskisoftwaretester.blogspot.com/) at [Ericsson Nikola Tesla](http://www.ericsson.com/hr/).

Every time about ten testers and developers meet and talk about testing and software development in general. This time there were twelve of us. We started by introductions. I gave a short [Test automation at HomeSwap.com](/test-automation-at-homeswap-com/) talk and Karlo introduced [the Set Game](http://thebreretons.com/setgame/). We were playing the game and discussing what would be the best way to automate it. I have created a [set repository at GitHub](https://github.com/zeljkofilipin/set) where I will play with the automation these days, when I get a chance. For now the repository only has a few examples of a solved game and one failing test.

As usual, we ended the meetup with a lot of informal talks about everyday challenges.

A few of us brought the books we own and our little [library](https://github.com/zeljkofilipin/zagreb-stc/wiki/Library) continues! (I am reading [Testing Computer Software](http://www.amazon.com/Testing-Computer-Software-2nd-Edition/dp/0471358460) (2nd Edition) by Cem Kaner, Jack Falk and Hung Q. Nguyen.)

Of course there were pizza and soft drinks, thanks to [Asseco](http://asseco.com/see/contact/croatia/).
