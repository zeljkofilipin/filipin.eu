Yesterday I was at [Zagreb STC](https://github.com/zeljkofilipin/zagreb-stc/wiki) [MeetUp \#6](http://www.meetup.com/SoftwareTestingClub/events/77674282/). As always, it was organized by [Karlo Šmid](http://zagorskisoftwaretester.blogspot.com/) at [Ericsson Nikola Tesla](http://www.ericsson.com/hr/).

Another great meetup. As usual, there were about 10 of us. Most of us were testers but there were a few developers too. We started with introductions. Everybody said a few words about themselves, what they do in life and what they were testing in the last week. We thought that would take 10-20 minutes, but the introductions were frequently interrupted with a lot of questions, so it lasted for about 90 minutes.

The pizza came about that time so we had a short break. After the break [Manuel Vidonis](https://twitter.com/vidonism) gave an introduction to software testing talk ([pdf](https://dl.dropbox.com/u/81045640/Testiranje%20programske%20podrske.pdf), [slides](https://dl.dropbox.com/u/81045640/Testiranje%20programske%20podrske_ppt.pdf), both in Croatian). His talk was also frequently interrupted with a lot of questions, so it lasted until the end of the meetup. In the last few minutes Manuel talked about [Traffic Manager](http://www.windowsphone.com/en-us/store/app/traffic-manager/e0e39137-3f57-42a1-8dd8-a7029effc0c0), Windows Phone game that he is developing.

I brought the books that I own and our little [library](https://github.com/zeljkofilipin/zagreb-stc/wiki/Library) continues! (I am still reading [Testing Computer Software](http://www.amazon.com/Testing-Computer-Software-2nd-Edition/dp/0471358460) (2nd Edition) by Cem Kaner, Jack Falk and Hung Q. Nguyen.)

Of course there were pizza and soft drinks, thanks to Ericsson Nikola Tesla.
