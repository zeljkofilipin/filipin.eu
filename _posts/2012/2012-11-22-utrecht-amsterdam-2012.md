A couple of weeks ago I was in Utrecht and Amsterdam for a few days. I was there to work with [MediaWiki](http://www.mediawiki.org/) (wiki software that Wikipedia uses) developers [Antoine "hashar" Musso](https://github.com/hashar) and [Timo Tijhof (Krinkle)](http://about.me/timotijhof). We also attended [Wikimedia Conferentie Nederland 2012](http://www.wikimediaconferentie.nl/) and [Wikimedia Nederland Hackathon 2012](https://nl.wikimedia.org/wiki/Hackathon_2012/en).

On Wednesday afternoon and Thursday we were working from Wikimedia Nederland office in Utrecht.

On Friday we attened the conference in Utrecht. One of three [tracks](https://nl.wikimedia.org/wiki/WCN_2012) was almost completely in English. The talks were pretty interesting. Since I am not really familiar with MediaWiki, the most interesting talk for me was [Jimmy Wales](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jimmy_Wales) (Wikipedia co-founder) keynote.

On Saturday we traveled to Amsterdam for the hackathon. Since I am pretty new to both the [Wikimedia Foundation](http://wikimediafoundation.org/wiki/Home) (a non-profit foundation that runs various open-content wiki projects including [Wikipedia](http://www.wikipedia.org/)) and MediaWiki, most of the time while we were at the office or at the hackathon I was just trying to figure out how everything is set up. More specifically, I was working on [MediaWiki browser tests](https://github.com/zeljkofilipin/browsertests) or trying to [install MediaWiki locally via Vagrant](https://github.com/wikimedia/wmf-vagrant). I have found the bug in the installation script, [reported](http://lists.wikimedia.org/pipermail/wikitech-l/2012-November/064348.html) it and it got [fixed](https://github.com/wikimedia/wmf-vagrant/pull/3) in 24 hours or so.

The last hour or two of the hackathon we had a few short talks. I gave a talk on how to install MediaWiki locally via Vagrant and how [MediaWiki browser tests](https://github.com/wikimedia/qa-browsertests) are set up. Timo talked about how MediaWiki and jQuery use [TestSwarm](https://github.com/jquery/testswarm) to test JavaScript ([jQuery TestSwarm](http://swarm.jquery.org/)). We had a few more talks, but I forgot the details.

By the way, according to my experience, everybody in the Netherlands speaks English. And I mean everybody. I have asked a few random people in the street, store or restaurant if they speak English and I got "yes" every single time. And pretty good English by the way.

Another surprising thing is lack of cars, really good public transportation and a surprising number of bikes. I was told that there will be a lot of bikes, so I was expecting it, but the number exceeded my expectations.
