In early February I was at [FOSDEM](https://fosdem.org/2013/) in Brussels, Belgium. Since I am writing about it now, you can imagine how much I remember. I have some notes and I took photos so I will be able to write at least a few words.

The trip started at Zagreb airport where I have met [Vlatko Košturjak](https://twitter.com/k0st) so I was not travelling alone. Since we arrived on Friday, we have decided to go to [Friday beer event](https://fosdem.org/2013/practical/beerevent/), but it was way to crowded. Luckily, there were plenty of places in the near with beer.

I have started the Saturday with [How we made the Jenkins community](https://fosdem.org/2013/schedule/event/keynote_vibrant_developer_community/) keynote by Kohsuke Kawaguchi ([photo](http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:How_we_made_the_Jenkins_community_by_Kohsuke_Kawaguchi.jpg)). I have spent the vast majority of the first day in [Testing and Automation devroom](https://fosdem.org/2013/schedule/track/testing_and_automation/). The first talk I saw there was [Automating OpenStack Testing on Ubuntu](https://fosdem.org/2013/schedule/event/automating_openstack_testing/) by James Page. The room was surprisingly full for this talk ([photo](http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Testing_and_Automation_devroom_at_FOSDEM_2013_Photo_1.JPG)). It was followed by [BDD for Mobile using Calabash](https://fosdem.org/2013/schedule/event/mobile_bdd_with_calabash/) by Karl Krukow ([photo](http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:BDD_for_Mobile_using_Calabash_by_Karl_Krukow.JPG)).

Then I gave [How MediaWiki, software that runs Wikipedia, is tested](https://fosdem.org/2013/schedule/event/testing_mediawiki/) talk ([photo](http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Testing_and_Automation_devroom_at_FOSDEM_2013_Photo_2.JPG)). I have noticed the talk mentioned in [Fosdem 2013, coté code](http://www.agilelevelup.com/2013/02/fosdem-2013-cote-code.html) blog post (in French).

The next talk that I saw was [Scale your Jenkins build pipeline automatically to minimize test time](https://fosdem.org/2013/schedule/event/scaling_jenkins_for_fast_tests/) by Anders Nickelsen ([photo](http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Scale_your_Jenkins_build_pipeline_automatically_to_minimize_test_time_by_Anders_Nickelsen.jpg)). My notes say I was also at [Building, testing and deploying mobile apps with Jenkins & friends](https://fosdem.org/2013/schedule/event/building_testing_mobile_apps_jenkins/) by Christopher Orr, but I could not find any photos. The last event in the room was [Jenkins Developer Meetup and Birthday Toast](https://fosdem.org/2013/schedule/event/jenkins_dev_meetup/) by Kohsuke Kawaguchi ([photos](http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Jenkins_Developer_Meetup_and_Birthday_Toast)).

All talks from the devroom were recorded, but unfortunately the laptop that had the recordings was stolen.

I have ended the Saturday with [ByteNight](http://www.hackerspace.be/Bytenight_Bye_Bye_Garage) ([photo](http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:ByteNight_Poster_at_FOSDEM_2013.jpg)).

I have started the Sunday with [Measuring the Wikipedia Community](https://fosdem.org/2013/schedule/event/research_data_analyst/) talk by my colleague Ryan Faulkner ([photo](http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Measuring_the_Wikipedia_Community_by_Ryan_Faulkner.JPG)). I also saw [How to hack on Wikipedia](https://fosdem.org/2013/schedule/event/how_to_hack_on_wikipedia/) talk by another colleague, Quim Gil and [Has the GNOME community gone crazy?](https://fosdem.org/2013/schedule/event/challenges_gnome/) by Vincent Untz ([photo](http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Has_the_GNOME_community_gone_crazy_by_Vincent_Untz.JPG)).

The majority of the Sunday I was browsing the stands. The most interesting ones were [Aldebaran Robotics](https://fosdem.org/2013/schedule/event/aldebaran_robotics/) ([photo](http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Aldebaran_Robotics_Stand_at_FOSDEM_2013.jpg)) and [Pandora](https://fosdem.org/2013/schedule/event/games_pandora/) ([photo](http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Pandora_Stand_at_FOSDEM_2013.JPG)).

We also had Wikimedia meetup ([photos](http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Wikimedia_Meetup_at_FOSDEM_2013)), where I have met a lot of my colleagues for the first time. (I work from home.)

I have finished the day with [The Keeper of Secrets](https://fosdem.org/2013/schedule/event/keynote_keeper_of_secrets/) keynote by Leslie Hawthorn and [Closing FOSDEM 2013](https://fosdem.org/2013/schedule/event/closing_fosdem/) talk by FOSDEM Staff ([photo](http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Closing_FOSDEM_2013_by_FOSDEM_Staff.JPG)). Since the event is completely free (as in free beer) they do not know the number of people that was there, but if I remember correctly the estimate was about 7000.

And for the end, looks like I am not the only one that finds [little boy peeing](https://www.google.com/search?q=taste+the+coke+side+of+belgium) (into the bottle?) on a Coke machine funny. I hope somebody got promoted for that.
