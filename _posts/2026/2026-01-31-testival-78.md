This week we had the first ever [Testival book club](https://www.meetup.com/testival/events/313040352). About ten people came to discuss [Taking Testing Seriously: The Rapid Software Testing Approach](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/201116293-taking-testing-seriously) by James Bach.

We did our usual introductions, had some pizza and discussed the book. Less than half of the attendees started reading the book. Most of the people just started reading recently, so they have read only the first few chapters. I was the only one that has read more than half of the book. (I read for about thirty minutes every working day.)

The discussion was diverse. From different chapters of the book, to [ISTQB](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Software_Testing_Qualifications_Board) and finally to [AI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_intelligence) (of course).

We were a bit distracted by a big screen in the restaurant showing the [Croatia-Hungary](https://ehfeuro.eurohandball.com/men/2026/matches/details/202611010101060/Croatia-Hungary/) handball game.

I really enjoyed the discussion. Two hours were not enough. I hope we'll have more book club meetups in the future.
