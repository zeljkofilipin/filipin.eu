Last month we didn't have a regular Testival meetup, so we had the second [Testival book club](https://www.meetup.com/testival/events/313776699/). The attendance was lower than the [last time](testival-78). This time only three of us came. It is my fault. I organized the meetup on very short notice. March was really busy.

We were talking about [Taking Testing Seriously: The Rapid Software Testing Approach](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/201116293-taking-testing-seriously) by James Bach. I was the only one that finished the book. Other attendees (Karlo and Bojan) started reading, but didn't finish yet.

It was still a good meetup. Karlo wrote a blog post [An unexpected display of art](https://karlosmid.com/2026/03/an-unexpected-display-of-art/) about it.

During the last meetup, I didn't yet write [Taking Testing Seriously by James Bach and Michael Bolton](taking-testing-seriously). I also depended on my Kindle for highlights from the book. Looking for relevant highlights on the Kindle proved not to be fast, so this time I have printed both the blog post and all of my Kindle highlights. It made it much faster to find things.
